The Problem

You Didn't Start a Business to Run Errands for It.

Most founders don't have a strategy problem. They have a plate problem. The day fills up with the same repeat tasks: chasing the same follow-ups, re-entering the same information, answering the same questions, fixing the same small breakdowns. None of it is hard. All of it is constant. And every hour spent on it is an hour not spent on the work only you can do. You feel busy, but the business isn't moving. The fix usually isn't more effort or a new app. It's finding the one recurring task that's quietly eating your week and taking it off your plate.

FAQ

Questions Founders Actually Ask.

Straight answers before you request a spot.

What if my business doesn't have just one bottleneck?

Most businesses don't. The sprint is built to find the single highest-leverage one first, the one costing you the most time or money right now. If we spot more during the session, we'll tell you, and you can add a second sprint or fold it into a larger engagement.

What does "qualified" mean for the founding rate?

The founding rate is for active businesses with at least one clear, recurring operational task, not startups with no operations yet. We'll confirm fit on a short call before locking in your spot.

Will you actually build something, or just tell me what to do?

Depends on the fix. If it's something we can implement live, a quick automation or a simple workflow, we build it during the sprint. If it's bigger, you leave with a specific, written plan instead of a vague recommendation.

I'm not technical. Will I understand what happens?

Yes. Everything is explained in plain language, no jargon, no assumed technical background. If AI ends up being part of the fix, we'll explain what it does and why, not just that it's "AI."

What happens after the three founding spots are filled?

The founding rate closes and the sprint moves to its standard price. If you're on the fence, the fastest way to lock in $197 is to request your spot now.
